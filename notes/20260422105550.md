# Emotional restraint is most powerful at the moment it breaks
Ishiguro nearly ended The Remains of the Day with Stevens permanently emotionally buttoned up. Hearing Tom Waits' "Ruby's Arms" changed his mind — the song's  power comes entirely from the tension between a lifetime of stoic resistance and the single moment it gives way. Ishiguro recognized that one carefully chosen crack in Stevens' armor would be more devastating than either sustained repression or open expression alone. Stevens believed himself a stoic; the crack revealed he had been surface acting all along.
