# The moment to begin writing is a threshold, not a schedule

Ishiguro identifies the decision of when to stop researching and start
composing as one of the most consequential in the writing process. Starting
too early is damaging; starting too late equally so. He describes getting it
right with *Remains of the Day* as a matter of luck — arriving at the *Crash* at the precise
point when he knew just enough.
