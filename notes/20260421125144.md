# A novel requires months or years of incubation before serious writing begins
Kingsolver keeps a novel "cooking on the back burner" for a long time. She spends considerable time writing about the novel rather than writing it. This includes themes, plot, characters, life histories, research requirements, and scenes as they occur to her, without worrying about chronology. The beginning and resolution emerge once the architecture is understood. During this incubation period she earns the authority to tell the story.
