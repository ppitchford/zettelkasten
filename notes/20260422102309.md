# Solving the right problem unlocks creative immersion

Ishiguro's "Crash" — four weeks of total social isolation and 13-hour writing
days — was not a productivity technique but a psychological one. The obstacle
he identified was not lack of time but failure to fully inhabit the fictional
world. By eliminating all competing reality, he engineered the mental state
where that world felt more real than the actual one; the output followed.
