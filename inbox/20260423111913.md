# Ideas require emotional capital as much as financial capital 

Ideas start out small, weak, and fragile. To survive that early period they
need not just financial capital but emotional capital — good energy,
positivity, and resilience. The primary lever is the internal monologue:
external validation is unreliable over the long term, so the hunger for
approval must be replaced with a desire to grow.
